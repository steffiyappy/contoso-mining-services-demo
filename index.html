Exercise 1 · Market IntelligenceResearcher

When to do this: Right after the pitch. Open M365 Copilot at copilot.microsoft.com and click Researcher.

Use Researcher (Critique mode — the default) to scan mining services & epc in Indonesia, benchmark peers, and surface the moves a GM cannot afford to miss this quarter.

Task 1.1🔍 Researcher · Auto (Critique)

Scan the Mining Services & EPC landscape in Indonesia

In M365 Copilot, open Researcher. Keep the model picker on Auto (this is Critique — dual-model with self-review). Paste this prompt:

Research the Mining Services & EPC sector in Indonesia. Benchmark these companies: Petrosea, United Tractors / Pamapersada, Bukit Makmur (BUMA), Saptaindra Sejati, Thiess Indonesia, Macmahon. For each, compare market share, revenue growth FY25, EBITDA margin, strategic focus, digital maturity, and recent material moves (M&A, capex, new contracts, regulatory). Include market sizing 2024–2028 with the top three growth drivers. Cite every source. Flag where evidence is thin.
Notice the citations and the "reviewed by" stamp — Critique enforces source grounding before the report is shown.
Task 1.2📝 Word Copilot · 1-page brief

Turn the research into a 1-page GM brief

In the Researcher report, click Export → Convert to Word. Open the file from OneDrive. In the Word Copilot pane, paste:

Based on the content in this document, draft a 1-page GM brief for the General Manager · Operations at Contoso Mining Services. Structure: - Market headline (2 sentences, 1 number) - Top 3 competitive moves we must watch - Top 3 implications for Contoso Mining Services in the next 90 days - 1 question to put to the Executive Committee Tone: direct, board-ready, no jargon. Max 350 words. Use [PLACEHOLDER] for anything not confirmed in the research. End with assumptions + open questions.

Exercise 3 · PowerPoint — Draft & DigestPowerPoint Copilot

When to do this: Open a blank PowerPoint (powerpoint.com or desktop). Make sure 05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx and 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx are in your OneDrive.

3.1 · Draft a BCG/McKinsey-style strategy deck

Copilot in PowerPoint will draft a deck in the "one message per slide" style consulting firms use — grounded on your memo and KPI workbook.

Task 3.1a🎨 PowerPoint Copilot

Draft an 8-slide GM strategy deck

In the blank deck, open the Copilot pane. Type / to reference your two files (05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx and 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx), then paste:

Create an 8-slide FY26 Project Portfolio & EPC Bid Strategy deck for Contoso Mining Services, audience: General Manager · Operations and Operating Committee. Ground every slide on /05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx and the KPI tables in /02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx. Use a "one message per slide" McKinsey/BCG style: every slide has (a) a complete-sentence action title that IS the conclusion, (b) supporting evidence in 3 bullets or a small table, (c) one source caption at the bottom. Slide structure: 1. Title — Contoso Mining Services · FY26 Project Portfolio & EPC Bid Strategy 2. Executive snapshot — headline + 3-number callout + RAG status 3. "Where we are today" — KPI scoreboard table (Overburden Removed (Mbcm), Equipment Availability %, Strip Ratio (actual vs plan), Fuel Burn (L per bcm)) 4. "What's changing" — top 3 market forces 5. "Three priorities for FY26 H2" — one bullet each, with owner 6. "Where to invest first" — 90-day moves with cost / benefit 7. "Risks & mitigations" — table: risk, owner, status 8. "The ask" — one-page decision summary for the Operating Committee Tone: direct, evidence-led, no adjectives. Use [PLACEHOLDER] for any number not in the source files.
Task 3.1b🎨 Self-critique

Tighten the deck — McKinsey-style critique pass

Act as a McKinsey engagement manager reviewing this deck. For each slide, suggest a sharper action-title (a complete sentence that IS the conclusion). Flag any slide where the title and body do not agree. Suggest one slide we can remove. Reply as a 1-page review note, then apply your edits to the deck.
Why action titles matter: in a BCG/McKinsey deck the slide title is the conclusion ("Same-store sales drag is concentrated in mid-tier department stores"), not the topic ("Same-store sales"). The body just proves it.

3.2 · Digest a deck someone just sent the GM

A 60-slide deck lands in your inbox 20 minutes before the meeting. Copilot reads it for you.

Task 3.2🎨 PowerPoint Copilot

Summarise & interrogate an incoming deck

Open any presentation you've received recently (or use the deck you just drafted). In the Copilot pane:

Summarise this presentation in 5 bullet points. What is the single key decision being asked of the audience? What are the 3 numbers I should remember? Identify any action items, owners, and dates. List the top 3 questions a General Manager · Operations should ask in the meeting.
Direct from Microsoft's "Copilot for Business Leaders" page — this is the #1 PowerPoint use case for executives: turning incoming decks into a 60-second pre-read.

Exercise 4 · Excel Power HourCopilot in Excel

The most-asked GM question: "Show me what's actually happening in the data — without me writing a formula." Excel Copilot now does this in four flavours. We will run all four on 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx.

4.1 · Get the insights — one prompt

Task 4.1📊 Excel Copilot · Chat

Ask Copilot to find what matters in 12 months of KPIs

Open 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx from OneDrive. Open the Copilot pane (Home → Copilot). On the Monthly_KPIs tab, paste:

Analyse the Monthly_KPIs tab. For each KPI (Overburden Removed (Mbcm), Equipment Availability %, Strip Ratio (actual vs plan), Fuel Burn (L per bcm)): - Identify the best- and worst-performing location - Spot any sustained downward trend (2+ months) - Flag any single-month outliers worth a phone call Then compare against Targets_vs_Actual. Which KPI has the largest gap to FY26 target, and which location is dragging it the most? Show results as a 2-line executive summary, then a RAG status table by location.

4.2 · "Edit" — surgical, formula-safe changes

Task 4.2✏️ Excel Copilot · Edit (NEW)

Make a precise change without rewriting the workbook

In the Copilot pane, switch to Edit mode. Highlight the Status column on the Targets_vs_Actual tab and ask:

In this column, replace the emoji status with a plain-text 1-word status (Green / Amber / Red) and add conditional formatting matching the colours. Do not change any other cell. Show me the diff before applying.
Why GMs love Edit: they trust it. Edit shows a per-cell diff before applying — no surprise rewrites of working formulas.

4.3 · "Plan" — multi-step transformations with preview

Task 4.3📋 Excel Copilot · Plan (NEW)

Build an executive view in three steps Copilot proposes first

Switch the Copilot pane to Plan. Paste:

Plan: on a new sheet called "Exec_View", produce a pivoted summary — one row per location, one column per KPI — showing the FY26 H2 average. Add a "Trend" column showing ▲ / ▬ / ▼ vs the FY26 H1 average. Highlight the worst quartile per KPI in red. Add a 2-sentence executive read-out at the top. Show me the plan first. Let me approve. Then run it.
The "Plan" win: Copilot writes its own work order, you tick / un-tick steps, and only then does it touch the workbook.

4.4 · Analyst Agent — what-if & scenario modelling

Task 4.4🤖 Analyst Agent

Treat the workbook like a junior analyst

Open copilot.microsoft.com → Agents → built-in Analyst. Upload 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx. Try:

You are the General Manager · Operations's data analyst. Using the Monthly_KPIs sheet, model a scenario: what would full-year performance look like if the bottom-quartile locations on each KPI improved to the median by Q4? Show the year-end position vs FY26 target for each KPI before and after the uplift. Then identify the 3 highest-leverage actions: where a small move improves the group most.
Why Analyst Agent matters: it runs Python-grade analysis in natural language — regression, forecasting, scenario simulation — without anyone touching code. It is the closest thing to a junior analyst on call 24/7.

Exercise 5 · The Outlook Hour & Executive 5-Minute MomentsOutlook · Teams · Word

The lightning round — using your real inbox and meetings. Five things every GM does before lunch, each one is now a single Copilot prompt. We start in Outlook because that is where every executive's day actually starts. Sourced from Microsoft's "Copilot for Business Leaders" page.

5.1 · Outlook — catch up on a long thread (use your real inbox)

Task 5.1📧 Outlook Copilot

"I just got back from a flight — what did I miss on this thread?"

Hands-on: open Outlook on the web (outlook.office.com) or desktop. Find the longest email thread in your inbox from this week. Open it. Click Summary by Copilot at the top of the thread. Then in the Copilot pane on the right, paste:

Summarise this email thread in 5 bullets. Who is asking what of whom? What decision is the thread converging on? List any open action items, owners and due dates. Flag where I have been mentioned by name and what is being asked of me.
What success looks like: in 30 seconds you know who needs what from you and what to act on first — without scrolling through 40 replies.

5.2 · Outlook — draft a reply with the right tone

Task 5.2📧 Outlook Copilot

Draft an executive reply — short, on-brand, signed off

Hands-on: still in the same thread, click Reply → in the body, click the Copilot icon → Draft with Copilot. Paste:

Draft a reply to this thread on behalf of the General Manager · Operations of Contoso Mining Services. Confirm we are on track on the headline ask, push back gently on the unrealistic deadline, and propose a 30-minute call this week. Tone: warm, direct, hormat. 4 short paragraphs maximum, no jargon. Sign off as Pak Surya Wibawa.
Try the tone slider: after Copilot drafts, click Adjust → set tone to Direct or Formal and length to Short. Compare. Pick the version you'd actually send.

5.3 · Teams — recap the meeting you missed

Task 5.3💬 Teams Copilot · Intelligent Recap

"What did the Operating Committee actually decide yesterday?"

Hands-on: open Teams → Calendar → click any past meeting that was recorded or transcribed → Recap tab. In the Copilot box at the top of the recap, paste:

Summarise this meeting for someone who could not attend. Produce: (1) the 3 decisions taken, (2) the 5 open action items with owner + due date, (3) the topic that took most airtime and why, (4) anyone who pushed back strongly and on what, (5) what was promised to me by name.
Why GMs love this: they reclaim the meetings they skip — they get the decisions, the politics, and the asks of them, in 60 seconds.

5.4 · Word — interrogate a long document

Task 5.4📝 Word Copilot

"Find me the risk in this 80-page board pack"

Hands-on: open 05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx from your OneDrive. Click the Copilot icon in the ribbon. In the Copilot pane, paste:

Read this document end-to-end. Identify: (1) the 3 critical risks, (2) anything inconsistent or contradicted across sections, (3) anything I would expect a General Manager · Operations memo to cover that is missing, (4) the 3 questions I should put back to the author. Quote the exact sentence each finding is anchored to.

5.5 · PowerPoint — pre-read in 60 seconds

Task 5.5🎨 PowerPoint Copilot

"What is this 60-slide deck actually asking of me?"

Hands-on: open the deck you generated in Exercise 3 — or any deck a colleague sent you. Open the Copilot pane. Paste:

Summarise this presentation in 5 bullets. What is the single decision being asked of the audience? What 3 numbers should I remember? List action items with owners. Then list the top 3 questions a General Manager · Operations should ask in the room.
The pattern across all five: the GM does not learn a feature — they learn a single sentence: "Copilot, summarise / draft / interrogate this." Every Microsoft 365 surface answers to it.

6.1 · Cowork-driven personalised GM cascade

Forget Word's Mailings ribbon — let Cowork do the merge, draft each monthly cascade letter — production tonnes, equipment availability, HSE focus, and one specific 30-day ask per site uniquely tuned to that recipient, and queue them as Outlook drafts for the GM to review and send.

Task 6.1👥 Cowork · multi-step

One prompt → 14 individually-tuned letters → 14 Outlook drafts

In M365 Copilot, open Cowork. Reference both files with /. Paste:

Cowork — run a monthly project cascade to 14 site superintendents, project managers and HSE leads for the General Manager · Operations of Contoso Mining Services. Sources: - Recipient list: /03 Contoso_Mail_Merge_Recipients.xlsx (14 rows: First Name, Full Name, Location, Role, Email, Performance Band, Key Highlight) - Brand & tone: /04 Contoso_Brand_Guidelines.docx (use the "Approved GM Communications Template" exactly) For each row in the recipient list: 1. Write a 180-word personal letter using the approved template — a monthly cascade letter — production tonnes, equipment availability, HSE focus, and one specific 30-day ask per site. Tune the body to the recipient's Performance Band: - "Top Quartile" → recognition-led, ask them to mentor a neighbouring site. - "On Track" → acknowledge + push on one specific stretch goal relevant to General Manager · Operations. - "Watch" → direct, supportive, name one specific 30-day fix. 2. Reference the recipient's Key Highlight verbatim, in the second paragraph. 3. Sign off as Pak Surya Wibawa, General Manager · Operations, Contoso Mining Services. 4. Create the email in Outlook as a DRAFT (do not send) addressed to the recipient's Email column. Subject: "[First Name], a quick note on this quarter at [Location]". After all 14 drafts are queued, give me a summary table: Recipient · Location · Band · Subject preview · Draft link. Pause and confirm before sending any of them.
Why Cowork beats classic Mail Merge: classic merge inserts the same body with field substitutions. Cowork writes a different body for each recipient — and stops at "Drafts" so the GM signs off before anything is sent.

Task 7🚀 Industry-specific GM power move

One prompt, one decision pack, fully grounded

Act as the Bid Manager supporting the General Manager · Operations of Contoso Mining Services. We are responding to a gold mining client's 240-page EPC RFP for an open-pit overburden + ore-haul package, 36-month duration, fleet ~120 units, location East Kalimantan. Submission due in 14 days. WIN-THEMES (1 page) - Client's stated priorities (safety, cost, schedule, ESG) — ranked - Our 3 highest-impact win-themes with specific evidence (project / KPI) - Our 2 likely competitor weaknesses to highlight (factually) 2. BID EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (Word doc, 2 pages) - Strong opening line referencing the client's own language - Why us (3 reasons, evidence-anchored) - Schedule & mobilisation approach - Safety & ESG commitment - Pricing positioning (without the number — that's separate) - Closing call-to-action 3. COMPLIANCE MATRIX STARTER (table) - Pull every numbered RFP requirement from the spec - Map to: Compliant / Compliant-with-comment / Non-compliant - Pre-fill our standard responses where we have them - Flag the 5 most contentious items for legal review 4. RISK REGISTER (top 10) - Operational, commercial, HSE, regulatory, weather/seasonal - For each: probability, impact, mitigation, owner Mark every assumption [ASSUMPTION:...]. Use precise mining-services terminology (Mbcm, strip ratio, OEE, TKDN, TRIR). Do not invent project history.
The artefact: Four bid-ready artefacts — win-themes, exec summary, compliance matrix, risk register.
For the GM in the room: this is the use case worth piloting first. It maps 1:1 to the most expensive analyst hours your team spends every quarter — and Copilot does it in minutes, grounded on your own files.

Tips Prompt — untuk semua surface Copilot

Kerangka prompt GCSE

Goal — apa yang saya inginkan di akhir (brief, deck, dashboard) Context — untuk siapa, situasinya, batasannya Source — file atau data yang saya rujuk (gunakan / untuk reference) Expectations — format, panjang, apa yang dimasukkan, apa yang TIDAK dimasukkan Dua kalimat untuk ditambahkan ke setiap prompt: "Jangan mengada-ada. Gunakan [PLACEHOLDER] untuk apa pun yang tidak ada di sumber." "Akhiri dengan asumsi yang dibuat dan pertanyaan terbuka."

Prompt siap pakai untuk Contoso Mining

Researcher · Sector scan
Riset [topik] di sektor mining services & epc di Indonesia. Bandingkan [3-4 peer]. Cantumkan semua sumber. Tandai bagian yang buktinya tipis.
Excel · Tabel KPI
Analisa [nama tab] di workbook ini. Untuk setiap lokasi hitung [metric]. Tandai kuartil terbawah dengan warna merah. Tambahkan executive read-out 2 kalimat di paling atas.
Word · Brief untuk GM
Susun brief 1 halaman untuk General Manager · Operations. Cakup: headline, tiga temuan, tiga aksi, permintaan ke committee. Maksimal 350 kata. Jangan mengada-ada data.
PowerPoint · Gaya BCG
Susun deck strategi 8 slide tentang [topik]. Satu pesan per slide. Action title (kalimat lengkap). Body membuktikan title dalam 3 bullet. Source caption di bawah setiap slide.
