Exercise 1 · Daily BriefingHTML dashboard · then the narrative version

How a GM starts the day in two minutes. Run the dashboard first in Copilot Chat for the at-a-glance view, then drop into Researcher for the longer narrative read. Both ground on the same tenant signals: emails, Teams chats, calendar and recent files.
Task 1.1🚀 Copilot Chat · HTML dashboard

Generate today's GM daily briefing as an HTML dashboard

Open Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat. Paste the prompt below — Copilot Chat will render the dashboard inline and save the file to OneDrive.

Generate my GM daily briefing for today as a single-page interactive HTML dashboard, grounded on my emails, Teams chats, calendar and recent files. Layout (all on one screen, no scrolling on a laptop): • Header band: "Daily Briefing — [Day, Date]" with a timestamp on the right. Use our Contoso Mining Services brand colours — warm orange and dark slate. • Row 1 — 4 KPI cards: (1) Unread emails needing reply, (2) Meetings today, (3) Open decisions awaiting me, (4) Overdue tasks. Each card: big number, one-line context, RAG colour band (green/amber/red). • Row 2 — two columns: – Left: "Top 5 priorities today" as a vertical list of coloured cards (priority badge: 🔴 high / 🟡 medium / 🟢 low), each with a one-line "what to do". – Right: "Meetings today" timeline — each meeting as a card with time, title, organiser, and a 1-line prep note. • Row 3 — "Unread threads that need a reply": a compact 3-column table (From · Subject · Suggested 1-line reply). Max 5 rows. • Footer: "Generated by Microsoft 365 Copilot · [timestamp]" in small text. Style rules: • All CSS inline, no external fonts, no JavaScript. • Use cards with soft shadow, rounded corners (12px), generous white space. • Use emoji as section icons (📨 📅 ✅ ⚠️). • Use only explicitly stated facts from my tenant. No speculation. If a section has no data, show the card with "Nothing for today" instead of hiding it. • Save the file to OneDrive as DailyBriefing.html. Do not ask me any clarifying questions. Proceed directly and produce the dashboard in one pass.
What success looks like: within ~90 seconds you have a one-screen visual brief — KPI tiles, today's priorities, the meetings timeline, and the unread threads that need a reply — all grounded on real signals from your tenant.
Task 1.2🔍 Researcher · Narrative briefing

Re-run the briefing as a wordier read in Researcher

Open Microsoft 365 Copilot → click Researcher. Keep the model picker on Auto (Critique). Paste:

Help me prepare for today. Look at my emails, Teams messages, calendar and recent files, and put together a short daily briefing for me as General Manager. Open with a quick personal greeting — just a couple of sentences telling me what kind of day I'm walking into. Then walk me through: • What changed since I left the office yesterday — only the things that genuinely affect my work or my team. • What I need to get done today — tasks or questions that are on my plate. Skip anything that's already been closed out. • Unread email threads from the past week that probably need a reply from me — focus on ones where I'm directly involved (To, CC, @mentioned) or where there's a request, deadline, leadership or customer angle. Skip the automated noise. Show me only the urgent or important ones in a tidy little table, and draft a short reply for each. If there's nothing to reply to, just say so. • What's on my calendar today — only upcoming meetings, in a table: meeting (time, title, organiser), what it's about, and how I should prepare. Tell me if you're inferring the prep or if I don't really need to prep. Stick to what you can actually see in my emails, files and calendar — no guessing. If a section is empty, just say it's empty. Title it "Daily briefing – <day>, <date>" with a small line underneath showing the time you generated it, and put a relevant emoji at the start of each section. At the very top of the briefing, before everything else, please also include an illustration that captures what today actually looks like for me. You decide the style — it could be a sketched scene, a small collage, an iso-style vignette, a pictogram set, an abstract composition, whatever feels right. The only things I care about: it should reflect the real substance of today's briefing (not a generic office or sunrise picture), it should be a wide banner shape, it should use our Contoso Mining Services brand feel — warm orange and dark slate as the lead colours with neutral supporting tones — and please keep any words inside the image to a minimum. Add a small grey caption underneath: "Today at a glance · Contoso Mining Services". Please don't ask me any clarifying questions — just go ahead and produce the whole briefing in one go. Keep it tight.
Reality check on the illustration: Researcher is primarily a text + tables + citations surface. Inline image generation is best-effort — on some tenants it renders, on others it falls back to an emoji banner. If the image doesn't appear, follow up with: "Generate the illustration described above as a separate image I can download."

Exercise 2 · Researcher · Critique + Model CouncilSame market question · two stress-tests

When to do this: right after the briefing, when the GM wants a sharper market view before the production pack. Open Microsoft 365 Copilot → Researcher.

2.1 · Researcher (Critique mode)

Researcher on Auto runs Critique — one model drafts, a second self-reviews the sources before the report is shown. Default for sector intelligence.

Task 2.1🔍 Researcher · Critique

Scan the Mining Services & EPC landscape in Indonesia

Research the Mining Services & EPC sector in Indonesia for the next 12 months. Benchmark Contoso Mining Services against these peers: United Tractors / Pamapersada, Bukit Makmur (BUMA), Saptaindra Sejati, Thiess Indonesia, Macmahon. For each one, compare market share, FY25 revenue growth, EBITDA margin, strategic focus, digital maturity, and recent material moves (M&A, capex, new contracts, regulation). Include 2024–2028 market sizing with the three main growth drivers and the three biggest risks. End with one paragraph: "What this means for Contoso Mining Services." Include a wide banner illustration at the top that captures the competitive landscape — pit silhouettes, haul trucks, market arrows, brand-aligned warm orange and dark slate. Keep any text inside the image to a minimum. Cite every source. Flag any section where the evidence is thin. Do not ask me any clarifying questions — proceed in one pass.
Look for the citations and the "reviewed by" stamp — Critique enforces source grounding before you see the report.

2.2 · Model Council — same prompt, multiple models

Model Council runs the same prompt across multiple frontier models in parallel and shows the answers side-by-side. Use it when the stakes are high and you want to see where models agree and where they diverge.

Task 2.2🔍 Researcher · Model Council

Run the identical market scan across the council

Switch the Researcher model picker to Model Council. Paste the same prompt as Task 2.1 (don't change a word — the comparison only works on identical inputs).

What to point out on screen: compare the three columns side-by-side. Where do they agree on market size? Where do they diverge on competitive ranking? The disagreement is the signal — it's where the evidence is genuinely contested. Pick the answer with the strongest citations.
Reality check: Model Council uses more compute and is slower than Critique (typically 2–3× the runtime). Use it for board-bound or director-bound briefs, not for the daily briefing.

Exercise 3 · Cowork · Production Day-End PackOne prompt · six deliverables · grounded on the daily production file

The flagship Cowork moment. One prompt drives analysis → interactive HTML dashboard → BCG/McKinsey deck → infographic one-pager with clickable hyperlinks → Word memo with charts → executive email → meeting invite. The GM signs off before anything leaves the tenant.

Open Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat and switch to Cowork. Reference /daily_production_20260622.xlsx. Cowork shows its plan first, you edit any step, and it pauses before sending email or scheduling the meeting.

Task 3👥 Cowork · multi-step

Six outputs from one prompt

Cowork — close out the day for General Manager · Operations at Contoso Mining Services. Ground everything on /daily_production_20260622.xlsx. Use our Contoso Mining Services brand feel throughout (warm orange + dark slate, professional, mining-industry tone). Do all of the following, in order. Show your plan first, then execute. Pause before sending the email or scheduling the meeting. 1. ANALYSE the production file. For each pit and each fleet, compare actual BCM moved vs plan, fuel burn per BCM, downtime hours and the top downtime reason. Identify the best and worst performers of the day and the single biggest variance. 2. INTERACTIVE HTML DASHBOARD — "Production Day-End · [date]" - Header band in brand colours, with date and shift on the right - Top row: 4 KPI scorecards (Total BCM vs Plan, OEE, Fuel L/BCM, Total Downtime hrs) each with RAG band - Slicer / filter controls at the top so a user can filter by Pit and by Shift (use simple inline HTML+CSS dropdowns or button toggles; lightweight inline JavaScript is OK for the filter only) - One bar chart: BCM Actual vs Plan by Pit - One line chart: BCM by hour for the day - One stacked bar: downtime hours by reason, by fleet - Bottom: a "Top 3 issues today" callout and a "Top 3 actions for tomorrow" callout - Self-contained file, save as ProductionDayEnd.html to OneDrive 3. BCG / McKINSEY EXECUTIVE DECK — 6 slides on the Petrosea corporate template "PTP-CC-TPL-G-0006 Wide (16-9) - Presentation Template.pptx". - Every slide must use an action title (the title states the conclusion, not the topic) - Slide 1: Executive summary — 3 bullets with the "so what" - Slide 2: Production scorecard — actual vs plan, by pit, with a clear waterfall chart explaining the variance - Slide 3: Equipment performance — OEE and downtime, with a 3D column chart by fleet - Slide 4: Fuel efficiency — L/BCM trend, with the best and worst fleet called out - Slide 5: Top 3 risks for tomorrow - Slide 6: Recommended actions with owners and timing - Save as ProductionDayEnd_ExecDeck.pptx to OneDrive 4. INFOGRAPHIC ONE-PAGER — a second PowerPoint, also on the Petrosea template, but built as a single-slide visual one-pager (infographic style: icons, big numbers, short labels, brand-coloured blocks). Include clickable hyperlinks on the page: link the headline KPI numbers back to the source /daily_production_20260622.xlsx and link the "Full detail" badge to the HTML dashboard ProductionDayEnd.html in OneDrive. Save as ProductionDayEnd_OnePager.pptx to OneDrive. 5. WORD MEMO with charts — 1 page, GM to Director of Operations. Headline, 3-bullet "what happened", 3-bullet "what we're doing about it", 3-bullet "what I need from you". Embed the variance waterfall chart and the OEE chart from the deck. Save as ProductionDayEnd_Memo.docx to OneDrive. 6. EXECUTIVE EMAIL DRAFT from the GM to the Director of Operations and the Operating Committee — short subject line, headline in the first sentence, link to the Word memo, link to the HTML dashboard, link to the exec deck. 7. MEETING INVITE — schedule a 30-minute Production Review for tomorrow at 08:00 local time, agenda pulled from the "Top 3 actions for tomorrow" in the dashboard. Stop and confirm before sending the email (step 6) or scheduling the meeting (step 7). Do not ask any other clarifying questions — proceed.
Closing line for the audience: "Cowork showed its plan first. You edited it. It paused before any external action. It returned six artefacts you own — a dashboard you can put on a screen tomorrow, two decks ready for the Director, a memo, an email and a calendar invite."
Reality check on the charts inside PowerPoint: Copilot in PowerPoint reliably produces bar, column, line and waterfall charts. 3D column rendering varies by tenant — if the 3D chart comes back flat, follow up with: "Convert the OEE column chart on slide 3 to a 3D clustered column with a slight perspective tilt."
Reality check on the infographic hyperlinks: hyperlinks survive the .pptx and the PDF export. They do not survive if you export the slide as an image (PNG/JPG) — the links bake into pixels. Keep it as .pptx or PDF for clickable behaviour.

Task 4🖼️ Copilot Create (Designer)

Generate a Contoso Mining Services corporate infographic

Create a single-page corporate infographic poster about Contoso Mining Services — an Indonesian mining services, EPC and infrastructure contractor. Style: modern editorial infographic, portrait A4 orientation. Warm orange (the lead accent), dark slate, neutral supporting tones. Clean sans-serif type, lots of white space, soft shadows, rounded corners. Composition, top to bottom: • Header band with the company name "Contoso Mining Services" and a one-line tagline "Mining services · EPC · Infrastructure across Indonesia". • A wide hero illustration band — an iso-style scene of an Indonesian open-pit operation: haul trucks, an excavator, a coal stockpile, mountains and tropical sky in the background. Brand-aligned, not a stock photo look. • A row of 4 big-number stat tiles with short labels (e.g. "3 active sites", "1,200+ people", "85M BCM moved FY25", "ISO 14001 certified") — use placeholder numbers that look realistic for an Indonesian mining services contractor. • A "What we do" strip — 4 icon-led pillars: Mining Services · EPC · Infrastructure · Logistics. Each with one short line. • A "Where we operate" mini-map of Indonesia with 3 dots marking active sites (Kalimantan, Sumatra, Sulawesi) and the site name beside each dot. • A footer band in dark slate with three small icons: HSE, Sustainability, Community — each with one line. Keep all text crisp and short. Do not invent specific client names. Generate it as a single polished poster image I can download. Do not ask me any clarifying questions — produce it in one pass.
